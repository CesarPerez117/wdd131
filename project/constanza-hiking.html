Explore Constanza’s Most Breathtaking Trails

From the highest peak in the Caribbean to hidden alpine gems, these hikes offer unforgettable adventures in the heart of the Dominican Republic.

Pico Duarte

Towering at 3,101 meters (10,174 ft), Pico Duarte is the highest mountain in the Caribbean. The most popular route begins in La Ciénaga near Jarabacoa and takes 2–3 days to complete. Hikers pass through pine forests, rivers, and high-altitude valleys like La Compartición before reaching the summit. A guide and park permit are required, and mules are often used to carry gear. Sunrise at the summit is a must-see moment of national pride.

Agüita Fría

Nestled at 2,700 meters (8,858 ft), Agüita Fría is a high-altitude stop known for its cool climate and ecological importance—it’s the birthplace of both the Yaque del Norte and Yaque del Sur rivers. The trail is about 24.5 km round trip and considered challenging, with stunning views, birdwatching, and camping opportunities. It’s a favorite among backpackers and nature lovers seeking solitude and adventure.

Loma Río Grande

This circular trail near Constanza spans approximately 12.3 km with an elevation gain of 480 meters. It’s considered difficult due to steep climbs and rugged terrain, but the reward is pure serenity—lush green pastures, whispering pine forests, and panoramic views of the valley and Pico Francés. There’s a designated camping area at the summit, suitable for multiple tents.

Aguas Blancas

Located within Valle Nuevo National Park, this trail leads to the Aguas Blancas Waterfall—one of the highest in the Caribbean. The hike is moderate, with rocky paths and lush forest surroundings. Along the way, you’ll encounter unique biodiversity, cool misty air, and the soothing sound of cascading water. It’s a favorite for nature lovers and photographers.

Piedra Letrada

Just northwest of Constanza, this route takes you to an archaeological site at over 1,635 meters elevation. The trail winds through forested terrain to reach massive rocks carved with Taíno petroglyphs, believed to be part of a sacred sanctuary. It’s a moderate hike with cultural significance and scenic beauty, making it a must-see for history buffs and hikers alike.
